About Lebanon

Lebanon, officially the Republic of Lebanon,[d] is a country in the Levant region of West Asia. Situated at the crossroads of the Mediterranean Basin and the Arabian Peninsula,[14] it is bordered by Syria to the north and east, Israel to the south, and the Mediterranean Sea to the west; Cyprus lies a short distance from the coastline. Lebanon has a population of more than five million and an area of 10,452 square kilometres (4,036 sq mi). Beirut is the country's capital and largest city.

Lebanon

Placeholder Beirut

Beirut is Lebanon's vibrant capital and largest city, a historic port on the Mediterranean known as the "Paris of the Middle East" for its blend of modern and Ottoman architecture, bustling nightlife (Hamra, Achrafieh), rich cultural sites (National Museum), and significant financial hub, constantly rebuilding and showcasing resilience after conflicts, even facing major challenges like the devastating 2020 port explosion.

Placeholder Tripoli

Tripoli is the largest city in northern Lebanon and the second-largest city in the country. Situated 81 km north of the capital Beirut, it is the capital of the North Governorate and the Tripoli District. Tripoli overlooks the eastern Mediterranean Sea, and it is the northernmost seaport in Lebanon

Placeholder Baalbek

Baalbek is a city located east of the Litani River in Lebanon's Beqaa Valley, about 67 km northeast of Beirut. It is the capital of Baalbek-Hermel Governorate. In 1998, the city had a population of 82,608.

Placeholder Byblos

Byblos, also known as Jebeil, Jbeil or Jubayl, is an ancient city in the Keserwan-Jbeil Governorate of Lebanon. The area is believed to have been first settled between 8800 and 7000 BC and continuously inhabited since 5000 BC

Placeholder Jounieh

Located only 20 km north of Beirut, Jounieh is a coastal town with unique features. Ideally placed below a 550 meter mountainous promontory, it charms visitors with its landscape worthy of the most beautiful postcards. It even has a "U"-shaped bay, unique in the world.

Placeholder Saida

Sidon, or Saida, is the third-largest city in Lebanon. It is located on the Mediterranean coast in the South Governorate, of which it is the capital. Tyre, to the south, and the Lebanese capital of Beirut, to the north, are each about 40 kilometres away.

Placeholder Beqaa

The Beqaa Valley is a fertile valley in eastern Lebanon and its most important farming region. Industry, especially the country's agricultural industry, also flourishes in Beqaa. The region broadly corresponds to the Coele-Syria of classical antiquity. The Beqaa is located about 30 km east of Beirut.
